Your Site in Sixty Prompt

Copy this into Claude

This is the guided prompt that turns your Deep Work Interview into a live website. Paste your interview answers first, then paste this prompt.

You are a world-class web designer and brand strategist. I'm going to share my Deep Work Interview answers with you. Your job is to use those answers to build me a complete, single-page website that:

1. Tells the truth about who I am and what I do
2. Speaks directly to my ideal client
3. Positions me as the clear choice in my space
4. Makes it easy for the right people to take action

Use my exact words and phrases from the interview whenever possible. Do not invent positioning language. The power is in my real voice, not polished marketing copy.

Build the site section by section. After each section, show me the code and ask for my feedback before moving on. The sections should be:

SECTION 1: Hero. A headline that captures what I do and who I do it for. A subheadline that adds context. A clear call to action.

SECTION 2: The Problem. Describe the pain my ideal client experiences. Use the language from my interview about what frustrates them, what they've tried, and why it hasn't worked.

SECTION 3: My Approach. What makes me different. Not features or credentials, but the core philosophy or method that sets my work apart. Pull this directly from my interview answers.

SECTION 4: Who This Is For. A clear description of my ideal client so the right people self-select and the wrong people self-exclude.

SECTION 5: Social Proof. If I mentioned testimonials, results, or notable clients in my interview, include them here. If not, create a placeholder section I can fill in later.

SECTION 6: About Me. My story, told in a way that builds trust and connection. Use the personal details from my interview that make me relatable and credible.

SECTION 7: Call to Action. A final section that brings it all together with a compelling reason to take the next step.

Design guidelines:
- Modern, clean, professional aesthetic
- Dark background (#0A0A0A) with warm accent colors
- Mobile responsive
- Fast loading (no external dependencies except Google Fonts)
- Single HTML file with embedded CSS
- Use Outfit for headings, DM Sans for body text

After building all sections, combine them into a single complete HTML file and give me deployment instructions.
